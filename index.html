Lydia Chodosh (b. New York) is a designer and writer with an interest in language forms and narrative constructions.

She has worked as both an editor and art director in magazine publishing, and designed for various art galleries and museums.

Lydia holds an MFA from Rhode Island School of Design, teaches at Parsons School of Design, and currently works at The Whitney.

Orlando Porras (b. California) is an identity and editorial designer with an interest in personal vernaculars, athletic fandoms, and Chicano symbol-making.

He has taught at University of New Haven, Yale Prison Education Initiative, and Yale School of Art, and has worked with several leading design studios across the country.

Orlando holds an MFA from Yale School of Art, and currently works at the National Football League (NFL).

Rebecca Wilkinson (b. Montreal) is a designer and art director with an interest in design history, tools and processes as they relate to labor and organizational structures.

She has worked internationally as a producer and art director in commercial photography, and created identities and publications for visual artists and designers.

Rebecca holds an MFA from Rhode Island School of Design, has taught at both RISD and Parson’s School of Design, and currently works at Bloomberg Businessweek.

FULLSERVICE OFFICE (Brooklyn, NY) crafts identities and artifacts rooted in typography and image-making. We teach, publish, initiate collaborations and conversations. Our work is grounded in sincere and rigorous engagement with culture. We reject formulaic, surface-level strategies, and instead offer an editorial approach. Informed by broader socio-historical context, we engage the whole arc of a project from conceptual beginnings to material specificity.

The office is owned and operated by Lydia Chodosh, Orlando Porras, and Rebecca Wilkinson. Previous collaborators include Bloomberg, Condé Nast, Institute of Contemporary Art (ICA) Boston, NFL, RISD Museum, Yale School of Architecture, Walker Art Center and the Whitney. Our work has received recognition from AIGA, Dezeen, It’s Nice That, Sight Unseen, Society for Typographic Arts, The New York Times, and Tokyo Type Directors Club.
