Lydia Chodosh (b. New York) is a designer and writer with an interest in language forms and narrative constructions. She has worked as both an editor and art director in magazine publishing, and designed for various art galleries and museums. Lydia holds an MFA from Rhode Island School of Design, teaches at Parsons School of Design, and currently works at The Whitney.

Orlando Porras (b. California) is an identity and editorial designer with an interest in personal vernaculars, athletic fandoms, and Chicano symbol-making. He has taught at University of New Haven, Yale Prison Education Initiative, and Yale School of Art, and has worked with several leading design studios across the country. Orlando holds an MFA from Yale School of Art, and currently works at the National Football League (NFL).

Rebecca Wilkinson (b. Montreal) is a designer and art director with an interest in design history, tools and processes as they relate to labor and organizational structures. She has worked internationally as a producer and art director in commercial photography, and created identities and publications for visual artists and designers.
